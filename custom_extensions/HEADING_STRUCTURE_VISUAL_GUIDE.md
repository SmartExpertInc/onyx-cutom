## The Rules We Implemented

### Level 2 Headers (Major Sections)
- **Count**: 4-6 total per document
- **Content**: 300-500+ words each
- **Purpose**: Organize the entire document
- **Examples**: Introduction, Core Concepts, Application, Common Mistakes, Skill Practice, Conclusion
- **Visual**: Large emoji icon + ALL CAPS or Title Case

### Level 3 Headers (Subsections)
- **Count**: 2-5 per level 2 section
- **Content**: 150-300 words each
- **Purpose**: Break down content within major sections
- **Examples**: Specific pricing models, specific steps, specific mistakes
- **Visual**: Title Case, slightly smaller than level 2

### Content Grouping Logic
```
Instead of:
  ## Cost-Plus Pricing (level 2)
  ## Value-Based Pricing (level 2)
  ## Competition-Based Pricing (level 2)

Use:
  ## Core Pricing Models (level 2)
    ### Cost-Plus Pricing (level 3)
    ### Value-Based Pricing (level 3)
    ### Competition-Based Pricing (level 3)
```

## Testing Checklist

When you generate a new one-pager, verify:

**Header Count**:
- [ ] 4-6 level 2 headers (not 10+)
- [ ] 2-5 level 3 headers per level 2 section
- [ ] No level 2 header with only 1 level 3 subsection

**Content Depth**:
- [ ] Each level 2 section has 300-500+ words total
- [ ] Each level 3 subsection has 150-300 words
- [ ] No "orphan" headers with just 1-2 short paragraphs

**Logical Grouping**:
- [ ] Related concepts are under ONE level 2 header
- [ ] Level 3 headers break down the level 2 topic
- [ ] Clear parent-child relationship between level 2 and level 3

**Visual Appearance**:
- [ ] Document looks organized, not fragmented
- [ ] Clear hierarchy is visually apparent
